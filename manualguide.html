1. Medical Emergency
    Use this button for medical emergencies requiring urgent attention, such as serious injuries, heart attacks, severe bleeding, strokes, severe allergic reactions, respiratory failure, or unconsciousness.
  2. Violence Emergency
    Use this button in situations involving physical violence or assault, including incidents like physical assault, bullying, fights or brawls, threats of violence, domestic violence, or robbery and mugging.
  3. Fire Emergency
    Use this button in the event of a fire or smoke, such as building fires, forest fires, electrical fires, gas explosions, or burn injuries, especially if fire alarms go off.
  4. Security Threat
    Use this button in the case of security threats or dangers, including active shooter situations, hostage situations, bomb threats, armed robberies, or suspicious activities.
  5. Power Outage
    Use this button when there is a sudden power failure caused by grid failure, storm-related outages, electrical faults, power surge damage, or generator failure, especially when electricity cannot be quickly restored.
  6. Stranded
    Use this button when you are stranded or stuck and need assistance, or being stuck in an elevator.
